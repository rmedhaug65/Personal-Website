Weather Station & App

TheWeatherFrog iOS app is now in beta testing! Reach out if you'd like a demo. I plan to submit it to the App Store / Play Store by Halloween. The website is also live with limited functionality.

What I'm building

The goal is to remotely measure rainfall and wind speed in any given field. Instead of placing manual rain gauges in multiple fields and checking them periodically, I'm creating a digital station you can monitor from your phone in real time.

How it works (hardware & firmware)

I'm using a LILYGO ESP32 microcontroller paired with a SIM7000G LTE module. Firmware written in the Arduino IDE reads a DS18B20 temperature sensor plus weather instruments—anemometer (wind speed), wind vane (direction), and tipping‑bucket rain gauge—from the SparkFun weather kit. The station also captures GPS latitude/longitude. Data is sent to AWS over Wi‑Fi or LTE.

Data pipeline & app

Measurements land in Amazon RDS (SQL). I use API Gateway with AWS Lambda to expose clean API endpoints that the app and website query. The app also integrates OpenWeatherMap (forecasts), Google Places (geocoding/autocomplete), Mapbox (maps), and RainViewer (radar). Firebase handles user registration, sign‑in, and profile details.

Product build & enclosure

Here's the station's “brains” (minus the wind vane, anemometer, and rain gauge). I designed the enclosure in Fusion 360 to match the solar panel footprint and printed it on my 3D printer in PETG for outdoor durability. I'm currently designing a custom PCB and 3D‑modeling the production enclosure. Long‑term, the goal is an injection‑molded product with a board tailored to the station's needs.

Why I'm doing it

I wanted an end‑to‑end product build—from hardware to firmware, cloud, and mobile apps. This project has let me learn iOS & Android development, embedded electronics, and the backend engineering needed to make it all work together.
